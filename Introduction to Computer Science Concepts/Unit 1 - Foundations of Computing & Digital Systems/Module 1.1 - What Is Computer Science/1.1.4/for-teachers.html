Debrief questions

When looking at the map, why would a driver want to hide the 'water' or 'building' layers but keep the 'road' layer?

Sample answer: A driver only needs information that helps them navigate. Extras like topography or building heights are abstractions that would clutter the view without helping the primary task of driving.

How did the black box machine show the difference between what we observe and the underlying rule?

Sample answer: Observation gives us the input-output pairs (evidence), but abstraction allows us to describe the general rule (inference) without needing to list every single possible calculation.

Can you think of an everyday object that is a 'black box'? How do you use it without knowing all the internal details?

Sample answer: Common examples include a microwave, a car ignition, or a TV remote. We know the 'inputs' (buttons) and 'outputs' (heat, movement, channel change), but the complex engineering inside is abstracted away from the user.
